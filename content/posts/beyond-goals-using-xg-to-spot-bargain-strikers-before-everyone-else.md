There hasn't been a single statistic that has improved the effectiveness of data analysis in the sport the way xG has. What is xG? Essentially, it's the percentage chance that any given shot will result in a goal. This percentage is reduced to a decimal between 0 and 1. A lot of factors go into this calculation, in its simplest form its the distance from the goal the shot is taken, in its most complex its a mathematical formula which involves the positioning of defenders, attackers, and the keeper.

When a team is in dire need of a striker, the natural first stat to look at is goals; goals win games after all. Nowadays, with every team having its own team of analysts with gigabytes of data to come through, a more appetizing stat is a player's xG. 

Let's imagine we are a football club in england. We need to sign a new striker to replace our current strike. So lets say there are 2 strikers, both from the French league. Player A has an xG of 20 but has scored a whopping 25 goals in the league, leading to a price point of 90 million (Euros, Pounds...whatever you Europeans want to imagine). Player B has an xG of 15 as well, but only scored 10 goals in the league, thus leading to a 20 million price point. There are a couple of ways to think through. Now realistically the average fan is going to look at these two and go 25 > 10, and if your club is one of the big ones, 90$ million is a price you may have to pay these days. However xG shouldnt be thought of in a way that better the player the more they overperform their xG. Lets take a look at some of the greatest players of this generation.

For now lets ignore that differential and look purely at xG. The top two in xG are Cristiano Ronaldo and Robert Lewandowski. However one has a differential of +5.9 and the other -6.52. Both are just narrowly around their xG marks. 

Looking at Goals we can see that actaully the bottom 3 goal scorers overacheived by 10 goals in total whilst the 3rd highest goal score in Robert Lewandowski underacheived by -6.52 goals. This is to say that the best goal scorers aren't the ones overachieving their xG (unless you're Messi in which case logic does not apply), they are the ones creating the highest xG. At the level of proffessional soccer the ability to score a goal is relativley similar throughout players, that is to say although you may think Darwin Nunez has an inability to hit the target, his finishing is top 99.9% of people on this earth. You can trust that anyone at that level can properly strike a ball and place it on goal within reason. This turns xG into a true probability, just like a coin flip. If a player has an xG of  15 per season, just like player B, you can expect that given enough seasons that player will average around 15 goals. Its the same in which if you flip a coin 10 times you could get head 7 times and tails 3 times. Someone might then imply that whoever flipped the coin just has a knack for flipping heads, but let that person flip the coin 1,000 times LLN (Law of Large Numbers) will decide that you will be close to 50-50 probability. Now you may have some freak of nature (Messi) who has discovered a way to ensure heads is flipped 55% of the time, but only he will be able to do that. All this to say players who overperform will regress to the mean and players who underperform will return to their xG. So, to finish our earlier example, Player B is the smart, economical, and correct sporting decision.
